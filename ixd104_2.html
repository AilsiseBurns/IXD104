Travel apps

Below I will be looking at different travel apps for different purposes, to give me insight as to what is out and what is useful/helpful when travelling, so when I design/decide what app I to create I have an idea as to layouts, icons, colours etc. Below I will look at 2 apps, one that is rarely used, the other that is used on a daily basis by thousands of people in London.

I have decided to look at Waze first. Waze is a traffic app, that monitors traffic, delays, police checks and public alerts.

I really liked the colour scheme for this app, and how illustrative it is, with detailing to the 3D drawings.

Icons are simple but instantly recognisable. It is clear that a lot of thought has been considered when it came to layout, it is easy to navigate with a consistent illustrative theme throughout.

The home page is a map with an icon symbolising the users location, again it is simple, minimal yet effective as the text is kept to a minimum.


I really like the Waze app due to its simplicity in style and consistent illustrative them throughout. However, I feel the purpose of the app could have been improved upon, as only users who have the app can text in information.

Tubemap

The second app I decided to look at was the TubeMap map way app, used widely for undergrounds in London. I decided to look at this because it is used daily and needed if you live in London.

The app explains how to use the app however, could go into more depth.

The app shows the lines that run throughout the day/ night and the railway, meaning that the public have access to all transport routes.

The app also offers other functions such as a travel guide in London as well as other map way apps, such bus times in London and tube maps for Paris, New York etc.

The app offers various different features, and has a consistent theme throughout it.


However, the app is very text heavy and could be more creative with using the route colours rather then text and more symbols.


Further Travel App Research

What is an Icon?

An Icon is a visual symbol, and representation of an item which it signifies. Icons are commonly found on computer screens, options and windows. Icons can represent instructions, options, and programs which will allow the user to select by clicking on using a cursor. Icons are really useful in signage and bi-lingual situations, as they tend to be simple and recognisable, without needing words.

What is an Illustration?

Due to the fact that not only will icons feature in my app, but so will some illustrative work, I decided I would look into the actual definitions of what an Illustration is.

Image by Aya Devin

An illustration can represent a story, or an idea. Illustrations can be used for decorations, or visual representation of processes/concepts. Illustrations can be found in books, newspapers, brainstorming as well as in sketch books, and art work.

image by Javi Wolf

Illustrations can vary from realism/surrelism/cartoons and many more, as well as how they’re created; through paint/pencils/markers/ crayons/ink and most recently through digital forms. You can create illustrations on software such as adobe illustrator, photoshop, and sketch.

Brand!


Every App needs a brand, and what better way to do that then creating a name, monogram, word mark and visual marque. I used similar techniques that I learned from our IXD103 to help create a more effective brand.

First step was creating an app name, I didn’t want the standard like America’s most wanted or Crime in America, I wanted something that was interesting and different and would attract people, and something that isn’t to ‘wordy’ so I came to the conclusion of Infamous Crime, as the app is about crime, and I chose some of the most infamous criminals in America within the content.

I then brainstormed some images that I relate to crime in general mainly, not so much focusing on just America because the app could be broadened to a wider area. I wanted to have an idea of what could be incorporated in monograms and visual marques.

So I quickly brainstormed word marks, monograms and visual marques, and chose one of each to digitise, I didn’t want to spend a lot of time on the brand as it is an extension of the app but not the app itself, something to make it look more professional etc. I find that sometimes less is more especially when working on small scale as the images need to have good readability on a small scale.

For the word mark I had an idea on the type of font I wanted- slab serif- but not the specific font, so like IXD103 I decided to lay out all of the possibilities of fonts that I liked and chose my favourite.

So I chose to Slabo 27px, and I liked the kerning and how the lettering doesn’t take up as much space vertically.

Out of all of the Monograms I really liked the idea of using the letters to create a tool that is used in crime investigations, I did remove the serif at the top of the C as it didn’t look like a magnifying glass.

I purposely used this as a visual mark also, but elaborated it more, so I added a curved vector to partially complete the magnifying glass. I deliberately made the line thinner, so that the Monogram is is clear. I added a curved upside down triangle to create a shin effect.

This was the brand so far, before incorporating a colour scheme.

Charts today

  • Correlations- comparison

  • Decided/continuous/categorical

Guide to chart types

Bar charts

  • Vertical/horizontal
  • Stacked/100% stacked
  • Use horizontal labels
  • Space bars
  • Start y axis at 0
  • consistent colours
  • order data- alphabeticallys/sequentially

Pie charts

  • standard/donut
  • Dont have more than 5
  • dont use multiple pies for comparison
  • make sure data=100%
  • order slices

Line Charts

  • Include zero baseline if possible
  • no more than 4
  • Solid lines only
  • label lines directly
  • use right 1

Scatter Charts

  • Start y axis at 0
  • use trendlines
  • include more variable
  • dont have 2 trend lines

Bubble chart/map

  • Make sure labels=visible
  • should be scaled appropriately
  • dont have random shapes

Heat Maps

  • Use simple outline
  • Colur appropriately
  • use patterns sparingly
  • Choose appropriate data ranges

10 data dos and donts

  • One colour
  • Do order appropriately
  • Call outs- sections- icons throughout visualisation
  • Data- easily comparable
  • do use icons to reduce labels etc.- title each section
  • Dont use high contrast colours- subtle colour palette
  • Dont use 3D charts
  • Dont use chart junk-unnecassary illustrations/styles/shadows
  • Dont use more than 6 colours in single layout
  • Dont use distracting typefaces- italic/underline/comic sans
  • no more than 3 typefaces
